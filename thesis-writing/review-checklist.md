# Review checklist

Editing happens at two levels. Do **macro** first (fixing structure before polishing sentences that may get cut), then **micro**.

## Macro editing — structure & argument

- [ ] **Purpose is sharp.** Ch. 1 states the research question and *why it matters* (theoretical + practical significance). A fuzzy purpose flaws the whole work.
- [ ] **Each chapter flows from the previous one.** There is a visible thread: question → gap → method → findings → meaning.
- [ ] **Transitions exist between major topics**, not just abrupt section breaks.
- [ ] **Headings/subheadings consistent** in style and level throughout.
- [ ] **Literature review synthesizes, not lists.** Organized by idea; for each idea, common strands then departures; concludes with the gap that justifies the hypotheses. ❌ Reject any "Author (year) found… Author (year) found…" chain.
- [ ] **Hypotheses derive from the review**, are explicitly stated, and (TFM/tesis) include plausible **rival hypotheses**.
- [ ] **Methods justify, not just describe.** Says *why* the design/sample/instrument were appropriate; cites methodological references; covers internal & external validity.
- [ ] **Findings are clean.** Results only — no outside literature, no interpretation, no "this suggests." Descriptive → hypothesis tests (in hypothesis order) → post-hoc. Tables/figures for every numeric claim. ⚠️ Flag any sentence that interprets — it belongs in Discussion.
- [ ] **Discussion answers "So what?"** Relates findings to theory *and* to practice; doesn't merely restate results. Includes limitations and future research. This is the highest-value chapter — make sure it isn't skimped.
- [ ] **Conclusion gives closure** with a strong final sentence; speculation (if any) is clearly labelled.
- [ ] **Paragraphs argue, not just accumulate facts.** Each paragraph has a clear point, evidence or development, interpretation, and a link to the local section argument.
- [ ] **Streamline or expand**: any section bloated? any section too thin for its importance (especially Discussion)?
- [ ] **Bibliography complete** and in the required style; every source examined is included where the level requires it.

## Micro editing — line level

- [ ] Spelling and grammar correct (run a checker, then read by eye).
- [ ] **Consistent terminology** — the same concept is named the same way every time; defined at first use.
- [ ] Sentence and paragraph structure sound; no run-ons or one-sentence paragraphs carrying a whole idea.
- [ ] Variety in word choice (no paragraph leaning on one verb).
- [ ] Tables/figures numbered, captioned, and referenced in the text.
- [ ] Citation format consistent and matching the bibliography.
- [ ] Numbers, units, and statistical reporting consistent (decimals, p-values, CI conventions).
- [ ] Output format consistent with the project template. For LaTeX: labels, citation keys, cross-references, environments, and macros compile or are clearly marked as placeholders.

## Empirical-thesis weakness list (recurring failure modes)

| Symptom | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Discussion reads like a second Findings section | Add interpretation vs theory + practical implications; cut restatement |
| Findings chapter interprets results | Move interpretation to Discussion; keep results factual |
| Lit review = annotated bibliography | Reorganize by idea; end on the explicit gap |
| Purpose/objectives vague | Rewrite as a precise question + significance before anything else |
| Hypothesis tests reported in random order | Reorder to match the order hypotheses were stated |
| No rival hypotheses (TFM/tesis) | State plausible alternative explanations and how you rule them out |
| Limitations missing or buried | Give them an explicit subsection in Discussion/Conclusion |
| Method described but not justified | Add why-this-method, with methodological citations |

## Process reminders (finishing)

- Self-edit *before* any supervisor sees a draft — supervisors don't copy-edit.
- Leave time for the supervisor to read the full draft **at least twice**; committee members need it ~a week before the defense.
- Expect **post-defense revisions** — budget time for them.
- A quality thesis doesn't start and finish in one term; significant progress (data already being collected) should exist the term before you intend to submit.
