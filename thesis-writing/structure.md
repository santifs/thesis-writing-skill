# Chapter-by-chapter templates

Two templates. Choose by level. Both come from the same logic: question → known/unknown → method → findings → meaning. The TFM/tesis template is the TFG one made rigorous (explicit rival hypotheses, validity subtypes, operational definitions, dummy tables).

Always use subheadings throughout. Define each central term where it first appears. Use the institution's official template and section titles when available; the names below are portable defaults, not mandatory labels.

---

## Undergraduate thesis / TFG

Generic empirical undergraduate structure. Spanish equivalents are included for TFG work, but adapt section names to the faculty's official guidelines if they differ.

1. **Title page · Abstract / Resumen · Keywords / Palabras clave**
   - Abstract: ~150–250 words. Objective, method, key result, main conclusion. Write it last.
   - Often required in both the document language and English.

2. **Introduction / Introducción**
   - Broad framing of the topic and its relevance (1–2 pages).
   - **The research problem, stated as a question** with sub-questions.
   - **Significance**: who benefits — scientific contribution *and* practical/applied contribution.
   - Nominal definitions of central terms.
   - Objectives (general + specific) and/or hypotheses.

3. **Literature Review or Theoretical Framework / Revisión de la literatura o Marco teórico**
   - Theoretical foundations / overview.
   - Group prior work **by idea**, not by author: for each idea, common strands first, then departures.
   - Build explicitly toward **what is known and what is not** — the gap your work addresses.
   - End by deriving the research questions / hypotheses from that gap.

4. **Methodology / Metodología**
   - Description and **justification** of the design (cite methodological references).
   - Population / sample: how units were selected and why.
   - Instrument / measurement: operational definitions; any pre-test; reliability and validity.
   - Procedure: every step of data collection.
   - Data coding and analysis techniques (which statistics/tests and why).
   - Use **Materials and Methods / Materiales y métodos** only when that is the standard label for the discipline or institutional template.

5. **Results / Resultados**
   - Results **only** — no outside literature, no interpretation.
   - Descriptive/exploratory analysis first; then hypothesis tests in the order the hypotheses were stated; then post-hoc.
   - Tables and figures for all numeric information; qualitative results organized by theme.

6. **Discussion / Discusión**
   - Interpret findings against the theoretical framework from the literature review.
   - Practical implications (the "So what?").
   - Limitations of the study.
   - Future research directions.

7. **Conclusions / Conclusiones**
   - Brief summary of the whole work; conclusions tied back to the literature; a strong closing sentence.

8. **References / Bibliografía** — every relevant source examined or cited, according to the required citation style, e.g. APA, IEEE, Vancouver, Chicago.

9. **Appendices / Anexos** — raw data, instruments/questionnaires, code, extra figures.

## Master's thesis / TFM / doctoral dissertation, empirical

A proposal precedes the thesis; the thesis re-uses and expands the proposal's chapters I–III.

### Chapter I — Introduction / Introducción
- A. Broad introduction to topic and method (write this *after* the rest of the chapter).
- B. **Research problem** stated broadly as a question, with sub-questions, carefully explained.
- C. **Need for the research** — who benefits; applied and scientific contributions.
- D. **Nominal definitions** of central terms.
- E. **Context** clarifying the problem.

### Chapter II — Theory, Literature Review, or Theoretical Framework / Marco teórico
- A. Overview / theoretical foundations.
- B. **Literature grouped by idea** — common strands, then departures. Never string abstracts together.
- C. **Model** of the process, derived from the reviewed literature.
- D. **Hypotheses / propositions** — for each: restate its justification, explain derivation and implications, state assumptions, and **explicitly state plausible rival hypotheses** (substantive alternative explanations).
- E. **Scope of the study** — theoretical assumptions and the limitations they impose.

### Chapter III — Methods or Methodology / Métodos o Metodología
- A. Introduction — general description of method and design.
- B. **Design** — experiment / quasi-experiment / survey / etc., described in detail.
- C. **Sample** — universe, population, element, sample design, tolerance, probability.
- D. **Measurement** — operational definitions; indexes/scales; methods used to assess validity and reliability.
- E. **Analysis** — techniques and justification; nature of relationships expected (symmetrical/asymmetrical, linear/curvilinear, necessary/sufficient); include **dummy tables and worked examples** of the statistics.
- F. **Validity** — internal and external, with relevant subtypes.
- G. **Methodological assumptions** and the limitations they impose.

### Chapter IV — Findings or Results / Hallazgos o Resultados
- A. Brief overview.
- B. Results of applying the method; unusual situations; nature of the sample.
- C. Descriptive analysis (one-way frequency distributions on central variables).
- D. Validity / reliability analysis.
- E. Tests of hypotheses (ANOVAs, crosstabs, correlations…) **in the same order as the hypotheses**.

### Chapter V — Discussion / Discusión
Present only **interpretations** of findings, not opinion. Deal with both theoretical and practical implications.
- A. Brief overview.
- B. Discussion of method results + implications.
- C. Discussion of descriptive analysis + implications.
- D. Discussion of hypothesis tests + implications.
- E. Post-hoc analysis + implications.

### Chapter VI — Conclusion / Conclusión
May include the writer's opinion.
- A. Summary of the entire thesis (a few pages).
- B. Conclusions, referring back to the literature review.
- C. Implications — speculate about the broadest theoretical and practical consequences; **label speculation clearly**.
- D. Limitations (theory, method).
- E. Suggestions for future research.

### Appendices & References / Anexos y Bibliografía
- Appendices: bibliographic essay (sources searched; strengths/weaknesses of the literature), questionnaire/coding manual, raw data.
- Bibliography: **all** relevant sources examined, whether cited or not.

## Proposal vs thesis (TFM/tesis)
The **proposal** = Chapters I–III + appendices (schedule as a Gantt chart, facilities, budget, bibliographic essay) + bibliography. The **thesis** rewrites/expands I–III and adds Findings, Discussion, Conclusion. Clear instrumentation and consent with the ethics board (IRB / comité de ética) *before* collecting data — that approval can take weeks.
